Family Guide

Child Development Programs

Infant Program (0–12 months)

Focus on nurturing and care, including feeding, diaper changes, and regular naps.
Stimulate early sensory and motor development with activities like tummy time, singing, and soft toys.
Individualized care plans for each infant based on their specific needs and routines.

Toddler Program (1–3 years)

Language Development: Activities like storytime, music, and simple vocabulary games to encourage early speech and communication skills.
Fine and Gross Motor Skills: Structured play like climbing, crawling, and crafts to develop coordination and motor skills.

Our Approach

At Valley View Daycare, we understand that the early years of a child's life are critical for building the foundation for future success. Our approach is centered on the belief that children learn best when they are engaged, happy, and surrounded by people who genuinely care about their well-being. We take pride in our small class sizes, allowing our educators to give each child the individualized attention they deserve, ensuring that no child is left behind in their developmental journey.

Our curriculum is designed to spark curiosity and a love for learning, combining hands-on activities, outdoor exploration, and imaginative play. From arts and crafts to sensory play and early literacy, every aspect of our program is thoughtfully crafted to support the cognitive, social, and physical development of the children in our care. We emphasize the importance of building self-confidence, independence, and collaboration, as these skills are essential for success in school and life.
